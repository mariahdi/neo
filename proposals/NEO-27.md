# Community Garden Micro-Grant Proposal

**Submitted by:** Greenways Community Development Initiative
**Grant Program:** Neighborhood Resilience Micro-Grant Fund
**Date:** June 2025

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Families in the Riverside neighborhood face limited access to fresh, affordable produce — a gap that affects children's nutrition, household budgets, and community cohesion. The Greenways Community Development Initiative requests **$4,500** to establish a half-acre community garden on the vacant lot at 400 Elm Street, converting an underused space into a living resource that will serve an estimated **120 residents** in its first growing season. This garden will be planned, planted, and maintained by the community it feeds.

---

## 2. Understanding of Need

Riverside is a lower-income census tract where nearly **1 in 3 households** reports difficulty affording nutritious food. The nearest full-service grocery store is 2.4 miles away — a significant barrier for the roughly 40% of residents who do not own a vehicle. Simultaneously, the neighborhood has several vacant, city-owned parcels that generate no community benefit and contribute to blight.

Residents have already demonstrated demand: a 2024 neighborhood survey conducted by the Riverside Block Association found that **78% of respondents** expressed interest in participating in or benefiting from a shared garden space. The problem is not will — it is resources and coordination.

---

## 3. Proposed Approach

We will transform the Elm Street lot into a productive, accessible community garden through three sequential phases:

**Phase 1 — Site Preparation (Months 1–2)**
- Partner with the city's Land Bank to secure a two-year revocable use permit (negotiations underway)
- Conduct soil testing and remediation as needed
- Install four raised beds with accessible pathways meeting ADA clearance standards
- Establish a rainwater collection barrel system

**Phase 2 — Planting & Community Onboarding (Month 3)**
- Host two Saturday volunteer planting days open to all Riverside residents
- Distribute assigned plots by lottery, prioritizing households with children and seniors on fixed incomes
- Provide seed starter kits and a printed seasonal planting guide in English and Spanish

**Phase 3 — Stewardship & Harvest (Months 4–8)**
- Rotate a volunteer "garden steward" role among plot holders on a weekly basis
- Donate surplus harvest to the Riverside Food Pantry on a bi-weekly basis
- Host one end-of-season harvest celebration and planning meeting for Year 2

## 6. About / Qualifications

**Greenways Community Development Initiative** is a 501(c)(3) organization founded in 2018 to build resident-led solutions to food and environmental inequity in urban neighborhoods. Over the past six years, Greenways has:

- Supported **three** community gardens across the metro area, collectively serving 275 households
- Managed grants ranging from $2,000 to $85,000 with a clean audit record
- Trained **47 volunteer garden stewards**, 60% of whom live in the neighborhoods they serve

Our project lead, **Maria Santos**, has coordinated two prior garden installations and holds a certificate in urban horticulture from the state cooperative extension. We carry general liability insurance and will add the funder as an additional insured upon award.

We are grateful for this opportunity to put neighbors, not just land, to work.
