# Proposal: Technology Partnership with the American Red Cross

**Prepared for:** American Red Cross
**Distribution:** Proposals Group (Internal)
**Date:** June 2025

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Every year, the American Red Cross responds to nearly 70,000 disasters — from home fires to hurricanes — delivering relief to millions of people at their most vulnerable moments. The speed, accuracy, and coordination of that response depends increasingly on the technology infrastructure behind it.

We are proposing a partnership with the American Red Cross to strengthen that infrastructure: modernizing the systems that connect volunteers, coordinate relief operations, and ensure that the right resources reach the right people at the right time. Our work would directly amplify the Red Cross's capacity to serve disaster survivors, blood donors, and communities in crisis across the country.

---

## 2. Understanding of Need

The American Red Cross operates at extraordinary scale — managing over 300,000 volunteers, running the nation's largest blood supply network, and coordinating disaster response across all 50 states. That scale creates unique technology challenges:

- **Volunteer coordination** requires real-time communication tools that are reliable under disaster conditions, when networks are strained and timelines are compressed.
- **Disaster case management** depends on accurate, accessible data that field workers can act on quickly — often from mobile devices in the field.
- **Blood supply logistics** demand precision scheduling and inventory tracking where errors carry life-or-death consequences.
- **Donor and community engagement** must be sustained between crises, not only activated during them.

Legacy systems, siloed data, and inconsistent digital experiences can slow response times and create friction for both staff and the people they serve. Our proposal addresses these pain points directly.

---

## 3. Proposed Approach

We propose a phased engagement built around the Red Cross's mission priorities, beginning with discovery and concluding with sustainable, staff-owned solutions.

### Phase 1 — Discovery & Assessment *(Weeks 1–6)*
- Conduct structured interviews with program staff, field volunteers, and IT leadership
- Map current technology workflows and identify highest-impact friction points
- Deliver a prioritized roadmap with clear recommendations

### Phase 2 — Design & Development *(Weeks 7–22)*
- Build or modernize the tools identified in Phase 1, with continuous stakeholder input
- Prioritize mobile-first design for field usability
- Implement accessibility and security standards appropriate for sensitive beneficiary data
- Conduct iterative testing with real end users — volunteers, caseworkers, and donors

### Phase 3 — Launch & Knowledge Transfer *(Weeks 23–28)*
- Support a staged rollout with training and documentation
- Transfer full ownership to Red Cross staff, with no proprietary lock-in
- Establish a post-launch support window for stabilization

Throughout all phases, we operate as an embedded partner — not a distant vendor — attending your team's standups, respecting your governance processes, and centering decisions on the people your programs serve.

## 4. Outcomes & Measurement

We are committed to measurable impact tied to the Red Cross's mission, not to our deliverables alone.

| Outcome | Measurement |
|---|---|
| Faster volunteer deployment | Reduction in time from disaster declaration to volunteer activation |
| Improved case accuracy | Reduction in data entry errors and duplicate case records |
| Higher volunteer retention | Pre/post survey on volunteer experience with digital tools |
| Donor engagement | Change in repeat donor rate and digital conversion rate |
| Staff confidence | Tool adoption rate and internal satisfaction survey at 90 days post-launch |

We will agree on a baseline measurement plan during Phase 1 and report against it transparently throughout the engagement.
