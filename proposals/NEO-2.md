## 2. Understanding of Need

The American Red Cross serves millions of people each year across disaster relief, blood services, health and safety training, international humanitarian services, and support for military families. At this scale, the organization faces challenges that are both deeply human and operationally complex:

- **Coordination under pressure:** Disaster response demands real-time information sharing across volunteers, staff, and partner agencies — often in degraded infrastructure environments.
- **Donor engagement and retention:** Sustaining the philanthropic relationships that fund the mission requires personalized, timely communication at scale.
- **Volunteer management:** With more than 300,000 active volunteers, onboarding, scheduling, training, and recognition represent a significant operational undertaking.
- **Data integrity and reporting:** Funders, regulators, and internal leadership rely on accurate, timely program data to make decisions and demonstrate impact.

We recognize that the Red Cross has made significant investments in its own systems and capabilities. Our goal is not to replace what works, but to augment it — bringing targeted expertise where the need is greatest.

## 3. Proposed Approach

Our engagement would proceed in three phases:

### Phase 1 — Discovery & Alignment (Weeks 1–4)
We begin by listening. Our team will conduct structured interviews and working sessions with Red Cross staff and volunteer leaders to understand current workflows, pain points, and priorities. We will review existing systems documentation and map key processes before recommending any solution.

**Deliverable:** Discovery report with prioritized opportunity areas and proposed scope.

### Phase 2 — Design & Pilot (Weeks 5–16)
Based on Discovery findings, we will design and implement a pilot solution in a defined geography or program area. This phase is intentionally limited in scope to allow for learning and iteration before broader rollout. We will work shoulder-to-shoulder with Red Cross staff throughout.

**Deliverable:** Functional pilot with documented performance baseline.

### Phase 3 — Evaluation & Scale (Weeks 17–24)
We will assess pilot results against agreed success metrics, refine the solution, and develop a roadmap for organization-wide implementation. All documentation, training materials, and intellectual property developed during the engagement will be transferred to the Red Cross in full.

**Deliverable:** Scaled implementation plan, training curriculum, and transition documentation.

## 4. Outcomes & Measurement

We believe accountability to outcomes — not just activities — is what distinguishes a true partner from a vendor. We propose the following measurement framework:

| Outcome Area | Indicator | Target |
