# Neo — Competitive Landscape (research, mid-2026)

A scan of the market Neo competes in, to sharpen positioning and pricing.
Neo = a **private, modular, calm "personal OS"** — recipes, goals, wins, money
(Fixed/Loose/Float/Savings), wellness, body/health, career, trips in ONE app.
Promises: own your data (export anytime · never sold · never used to train AI ·
sensitive data encrypted), no streaks/gamification/surveillance, web + desktop,
$9.99/mo, 14-day trial.

> **Headline finding:** No mainstream app pre-packages named life modules
> (recipes + money + wellness + career + trips) *and* leads with "never sold /
> never AI-trained." Every privacy-strong rival sells generic flexibility you
> must assemble yourself. **That intersection — done-for-you + calm + private +
> whole-life — is an open niche.** It's also Neo's biggest exposure to misread:
> Neo is hosted SaaS w/ encryption-at-rest, NOT local-first/E2E/open-source like
> the privacy leaders, so privacy wording must be precise.

---

## Neo's category = the intersection of four things
whole-life + modular + **calm (no gamification)** + **private**. No single
competitor sits in that intersection. Neo is flanked on three sides:

- **"Life OS" builders** (Notion, Anytype, Obsidian, AFFiNE, AppFlowy, Logseq) —
  flexible & (some) private, but **DIY blank canvases**, not done-for-you.
- **Life trackers** (Exist, Gyroscope, Daylio, Finch…) — whole-life-ish, but
  **analytics-only or gamified**.
- **Specialists** (YNAB, Sunsama, Bearable) — excellent at **one** Neo module.

## Closest / most direct threats
| Competitor | Threat | Where Neo wins |
|---|---|---|
| **Notion + "Life OS" templates** ($0–10/mo) | Default, cheap way millions already do Neo's job | Cloud-only (no encryption); **DIY template assembly + upkeep**. Neo = batteries-included + private. |
| **Anytype** ($0–~$9/mo) | Owns Neo's privacy pitch — local-first, **default E2EE**, "own your data," no AI training; closest to whole-life via structured DBs | **Source-available (not true OSS)**, proprietary/less-portable format, and a **build-your-own toolkit** with a learning curve. Neo ships curated modules for normal people. |
| **Obsidian** (free; Sync $4–5/mo) | Trusted data-ownership incumbent; plain-Markdown; 4,300+ plugins can replicate Neo | Technical/power-user; **no pre-built modules**; E2EE only on paid Sync; closed-source core. |
| **Exist.io** ($6.99/mo) | Closest *philosophical* twin — calm, no streaks, private, whole-life, reflective | A **passive aggregator** that reads other apps. Neo is an **active input system** with first-class goals/wins/wellness/trips/career Exist doesn't model. |

## Privacy + whole-life set (the "own your data" crowd)
| App | Local-first | E2E encrypted | Open-source | Self-host | Whole-life | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Anytype** | Yes | **Yes (default)** | Source-available | Yes | **Yes (native DBs)** | Strongest balance; not true OSS; DIY structure |
| **AFFiNE** | Yes | Partial | **Yes (MIT)** | **Yes** | **Yes** | Docs + whiteboard hybrid; younger/less stable |
| **AppFlowy** | Yes (self-host) | Self-host only | **Yes** | **Yes** | **Yes** | **Local AI via Ollama** = best AI-privacy story; per-user pricing |
| **Obsidian** | **Yes** | Sync opt-in | No (open format) | n/a | Via plugins | Max portability (Markdown); DIY |
| **Logseq** | **Yes** | Sync opt-in | **Yes (AGPL)** | n/a | Via plugins | Only true OSS of the trio; DB rewrite stuck in beta |
| **Standard Notes** (Proton-owned) | Sync | **Yes (default)** | **Yes** | **Yes** | **Narrow (notes)** | Best default E2EE; not whole-life |
| **Joplin** | **Yes** | Optional | **Yes (MIT)** | **Yes** | **Narrow (notes)** | FOSS, flexible sync; notes-centric |
| **Monica** | No (hosted) | No | **Yes (AGPL)** | **Yes** | Relationship CRM only | Trust-based privacy; dev stuck in perpetual v5 beta |

**Takeaway:** several are *more cryptographically private* than Neo (E2EE/local/
OSS). But all sell **generic flexibility** — none ship named life modules. Neo's
edge is "already assembled + calm," not "more private than Anytype."

## Adjacent competitors (each owns ONE Neo module)
**Wellness / tracking:** Gyroscope ("health OS," ~$29/mo, score-driven), Bearable
(health/symptoms), Daylio / Finch / Habitica / Reflectly (**gamified — opposite of
Neo's no-streaks stance**), Stoic (journaling, iOS-only).

**Planning:** Sunsama (~$20/mo — shares Neo's *calm* ethos, but work-only), Motion
(AI auto-scheduling, $12.73+/mo), Reclaim (Dropbox-owned, free tier), Morgen
(privacy-strong — doesn't store event data).

**Money (Neo's budgeting module):**
| App | Bank-linking | Private? | Price |
|---|---|---|---|
| YNAB | Plaid/MX | No-sell pledge, but cloud + Plaid | $14.99/mo · $109/yr |
| Copilot | Plaid/MX/Mastercard/Akoya | No-sell; Apple-centric | ~$95/yr |
| Lunch Money | Plaid (+others) | No-sell/no-ads pledge | pay-what-you-want ~$50–150/yr |
| Actual Budget | none built-in (SimpleFIN opt-in) | **OSS, local-first, self-host** | free (~$1.50/mo sync) |

> ⭐ **Money insight:** nearly every budgeting app links your bank via Plaid and
> stores data in the cloud. Neo's **manual Fixed/Loose/Float/Savings framework has
> no bank-linking — a genuine privacy advantage** ("nothing to leak"). Market it.

## Where Neo genuinely differentiates (the wedge)
1. **The unclaimed intersection:** done-for-you modules + active input + calm +
   private. Nobody is all four.
2. **Batteries-included** vs. Notion/Anytype/Obsidian/AFFiNE/AppFlowy "assemble it
   yourself."
3. **Calm, no-surveillance, no-streaks** vs. gamified trackers (Daylio, Finch,
   Habitica, Reflectly).
4. **No bank-linking** in money = a privacy story the entire finance category
   can't match.
5. **"Never used to train AI"** is timely — AI-data-use is the 2025–26 battleground.

## Where Neo is exposed (be honest)
1. **Privacy claim is softer than the purists'.** Neo is hosted SaaS w/
   encryption-at-rest — NOT local-first/E2E/open-source (Anytype, AFFiNE,
   AppFlowy, Obsidian, Logseq, Standard Notes are). → Scope wording precisely:
   *"export anytime · never sold · never used to train AI · sensitive data
   encrypted."* Don't imply E2E/local you don't do. (E2EE + export-to-Markdown
   are roadmap items that would neutralize their main edge.)
2. **Price.** $9.99/mo is mid-high; rivals are free or cheaper (Exist $6.99).
   Justify via breadth + done-for-you + calm. **Add an annual plan (~$99/yr)** to
   match category norms.
3. **Breadth vs depth.** Each module faces a best-in-class specialist (YNAB,
   Bearable, Sunsama). Win on "good enough + unified + private," not on
   out-featuring any one. Keep modules focused.
4. **Trust.** "Private" is a claim; incumbents have SOC 2. A clear privacy/security
   page (and eventually an audit) closes the gap.

## Positioning takeaways
- **Lead with the intersection:** *"The calm, private home for your whole life —
  done for you, not built by you."*
- **vs Notion:** "No templates to build." **vs Anytype/Obsidian/AFFiNE:** "For real
  life, not power users." **vs trackers:** "No streaks, no guilt." **vs budgeting
  apps:** "We never touch your bank login."
- **Pricing:** add annual (~$99/yr) for LTV + category parity.
- **Possible future module:** a private "people/relationships" module (Monica
  proves demand) — only after core modules are rock-solid.
